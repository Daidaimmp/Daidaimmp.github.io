About Me

My research interests center on nonlinear dynamics and complex systems. I am particularly interested in understanding the nature of hidden attractors, how basins of attraction inherit the symmetries of the underlying system, and how machine-learning methods can be used to predict basin structures and their boundaries. I also think about the statistical differences between natural sequences and human language, especially in the context of hierarchical or algorithmic structure.

I have been fortunate to work closely with several mentors. At the ECSLab led by Associate Prof. Yu Liu (Beijing Normal University), I have worked on topics related to algorithmic information theory, nested hierarchy in sequences. I also conducted research at the Centre for Complexity and Complex Networks with Prof. Guanrong Chen, focusing on hidden attractors, basin structures, and the dynamical behavior underlying multistability. These experiences have shaped how I think about rigorous analysis, numerical experimentation, and the interplay between theory and computation.

I am always happy to discuss ideas related to dynamics, reservoir computing, and complex systems. I am also broadly exploring opportunities for 2026 summer research internships and 2027 PhD positions.
